Lake Nokoué is the reason a lot of people fall for Benin. It sits right on the edge of Cotonou, and yet the moment your boat pushes off from the landing, the city disappears and something older and slower takes over.

We split the day in two: the **history** in the morning, the **adventure** in the afternoon.

## Morning: Ganvié, the Venice of Africa

Ganvié is the largest lake village in Africa — a city of roughly 30,000 people living entirely on stilts above the water. It was founded in the 16th century by the Tofinu people, who took to the lake to escape Dahomey slave raiders (who, by religious custom, could not follow them onto the water). What began as a refuge became a way of life that has lasted five hundred years.

You reach it by pirogue. There are floating markets where women trade fish and produce boat to boat, a stilt mosque and church, and homes on legs as far as you can see. It is genuinely unlike anywhere else on earth.

## Afternoon: the Aguégué mangroves

Here is the part most guidebooks miss. A little further along the lake, in the **Aguégué** mangroves, there's a water base built for exactly one thing: fun on the water.

The menu, more or less:

- **Kayaks** through the mangrove channels
- **Hydrobikes** — pedal-powered water bikes, easier than they look and impossible not to laugh on
- A **floating obstacle course** of inflatables
- A **natural swimming pool** roped off in the clean water
- **Jet skis**, for anyone who wants the speed

![A guest pedalling a hydrobike across the water beside the mangroves](/photos/hydrobike-mangroves.jpg)

It is the single most joyful afternoon we run, and it works for almost everyone — families, couples, groups of friends. You don't need to be a strong swimmer; life jackets and a safety briefing come standard.

> Bring a change of clothes and a dry bag. You will get wet. That is entirely the point.

## Practical notes

- **Best months:** the lake is good year-round, but the water activities are most enjoyable from November through April.
- **What to bring:** swimwear, a towel, sunscreen, a change of clothes, and a waterproof phone case if you want photos on the water.
- **Timing:** a full day does both Ganvié and Aguégué comfortably. A half-day covers one.
